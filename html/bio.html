Hi I'm Lucas.

This is a picture of me acting natural as I lean against a brick wall.


I started my career in Customer Success, starting as an IC and ultimately at the Director level. I logged hundreds of hours in in-person user interviews with B2B customers. As an advocate the user I collaborated closely with Product teams to ensure designs would maximize the benefit to customers and reduce churn. I mapped customer journeys, analyzed touchpoints, obsessed over retention.



From 2015-2018 I also started, grew and sold a small business in the event industry, renting really cool vintage and custom-made furniture for weddings and special events. I worked hard, bootstrapped growth, and learned a ton about business strategy. It quickly grew beyond what could be considered a “side hustle” and I’m equally proud of its success and happy to no longer be involved.



I believe that “good UX” goes beyond common phrases like “delighting users.” Designers are responsible for ensuring that users are getting real value from the product, with minimal friction, in a way that minimizes client attrition, and they have to do so while impacting revenue for the company, because without that, there is no experience to speak of.
