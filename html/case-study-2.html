Strategy, Research, Experience Design for a proposed enhancement to the Panorama web app for increased user engagement

I conducted product strategy, user research, user experience design and interface design for a proposed new feature in FrontStream’s Panorama fundraising platform, with the intended outcome to increase frequency and depth of user engagement.

Company Overview

FrontSteam’s Panorama product is a web app that enables nonprofit organizations to fundraise via walks and other events. From the organization’s Panorama event page, individual walkers create personal fundraising pages and collect donations for the walk.

Problem

We observed that usage of the product dipped in the middle of the user’s event cycle.

The team observed that users were active around the time of first publishing their event page, and at the end of the event season, but there was little engagement in 3-6 months in between. This caused an upstream risk of stagnating revenue growth, low stickiness, and increased risk of churn. How could we improve the product to increase mid-event engagement?

Proposed Solution

I created an event comparison feature, intended to result in more frequent site visits, higher product IQ, increased satisfaction and deeper engagement with revenue-driving tools.

The event comparison tool would provide users a weekly update, comparing their results vs. the previous year, and offering condition action-items for how to address areas of stagnation or decline.

PROJECT OVERVIEW

I followed an interative cycle, borrowing concepts from the Lean UX methodology. My intention was to put business and user outcomes ahead of output, and test early ideas in low-fidelity.
